# UK Supermarket Analysis (2015–2024)

## Overview
An analysis of the shift in UK grocery market share toward 
discount supermarkets (Aldi and Lidl) during the 2021–2023 
cost-of-living crisis, combining market share trends with 
product-level price comparisons across 5 major supermarkets.

## Key Questions
- Did the 2021–2023 inflation surge accelerate the shift toward discount supermarkets?
- How significant is Aldi's price advantage on a like-for-like basis?
- Is the consumer shift toward discounters permanent or temporary?

## Key Findings
1. Aldi is ~50% cheaper than traditional supermarkets on a like-for-like basis, with the largest price gaps in drinks, baby products, and health products.
2. Discounters' combined market share doubled from 8.3% in 2015 to 18.1% in 2024, accelerating during the inflation surge.
3. The shift appears permanent — market share continued rising even after inflation returned to 2.5% in 2024.
